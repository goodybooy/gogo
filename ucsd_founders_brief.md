# UCSD Founders Brief

Compiled 2026-04-20. Source: LinkedIn post ("4 entrepreneurial goats from UCSD," Day 109 of Project 378).

> [!NOTE]
> YC batch codes used below follow YC's new four-batches-a-year cadence (W/X/S/F for Winter/Spring/Summer/Fall). "X25" = Spring 2025 (the letter X was picked because S was taken by Summer; Y Combinator leaned into the programming-variable pun). "F25" = Fall 2025, an October–December sprint with Demo Day on 3 Dec 2025.

<br />

## 1. Tom Zheng — clado (YC X25)

**Company.** [clado](https://clado.ai) (company entity: Freesolo, Inc.) is an agentic people-search engine. Users describe the kind of person they need — role, industry, geography, specific experience — and clado deploys "hundreds of thousands" of LLM agents to filter a 200M+ person-profile index and return enriched results with contact info. Think of it as Apollo/LinkedIn Recruiter, but you query in natural language and the output reads like a research memo. YC Spring 2025 batch (partner: Pete Koomen). SF-headquartered per the YC listing, though Tom is LA-based. Reported traction: ~20k DAU, thousands in MRR, and an Enterprise Networks product announced June 2025. No public funding round disclosed beyond YC's standard deal.

**Founder.** Tom co-founded clado (originally "Linkd") as a UCSD freshman in winter '24–'25. His co-founder Eric Mao (UPenn M&T) is a childhood friend; the two previously won Canada's largest data-science and physics competitions and ran a nonprofit together. Tom handles product and GTM.

**Relevance (quant lens).** Adjacent, not core. The product is a recruiting/BD tool, but the underlying pattern — massively parallel agentic extraction over a large entity graph — is the same pattern that alt-data vendors are increasingly using to build people/firm datasets. Worth tracking as a data-infra data point; not a counterparty or tool you'd plug into a macro book.

## 2. Miguel Monares — Jigsaw (a16z speedrun Cohort 005)

**Company.** [Jigsaw](https://speedrun.a16z.com/companies/jigsaw) is an applied research lab that automates the creation of reinforcement-learning environments for training foundation-model agents — "world generation, expert authoring, and evaluation" infrastructure that lets labs produce high-fidelity simulated tasks without hand-coding each one. Founded 2025, LA-based, 4 employees. Backed by a16z's speedrun gaming/AI accelerator (Cohort 005, check-size ~$750k) plus angels from Stanford SAIL and Anthropic. No priced round disclosed.

**Founder.** Miguel is CEO. He previously researched agents and evaluations at Stanford AI Lab; in 2024 he left school (UCSD per the LinkedIn post; he was in Tokyo by mid-2024) and bootstrapped a prior venture building simulated worlds for agents to >$200k MRR before rolling that experience into Jigsaw. Co-founders include Jongbin (Jay) Won (COO; ex-Polygon Labs, USC '22, prior agentic-gaming startup at 6-figure MRR) and Johnny Gonzales; Jack Determan is on the environments team.

**Relevance (quant lens).** Thin but not zero. RL-environment tooling is tangential to trading — the nearest analogue is simulator/backtester infra. If foundation-model RL-for-markets ever becomes real, shops building this layer will be the picks-and-shovels. Keep on the watch-list; not actionable today.

## 3. Sky Yang — Imagine AI (YC F25)

**Company.** [Imagine AI](https://www.imagineai.me) builds a "B2B content clone" for executives: the team spends ~100 pages interviewing a founder/exec, distills a persona report, then an in-house Content Engineer uses that voice-model to run the exec's LinkedIn/blog pipeline — posts, strategic comments, long-form. Pitched as "agentic Head of Content" rather than a generic AI-writing SaaS. YC Fall 2025 batch. Launched November 2025, SF-based. Reported traction (per the company's public materials): 40+ paying clients spanning pre-seed → public (named: Rippling, Corgi Insurance, Campfire AI), "$31M in revenue generated across clients," 431 inbounds for one client in a month. Those client-attributed numbers are self-reported — treat as directional.

**Founder.** Sky Yang (CEO) was UCSD's youngest-ever Associated Students President; he also founded a COVID-era nonprofit, Break the Outbreak. CTO Neo Lee came out of LLM-interpretability research at UC Berkeley.

**Relevance (quant lens).** None direct. This is a marketing-ops tool. Treat as a UCSD-alumni profile.

## 4. Leo (Songang) Lu — Foundation Capital

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Leo is a **VC partner**, not an operator. Profile below is written as a VC relationship card rather than a startup brief.

**Firm.** [Foundation Capital](https://foundationcapital.com/member/leo-lu/) is a ~40-year-old early-stage SF/Palo Alto firm (~$3B+ AUM across funds). Leo sits on the enterprise team and is publicly focused on AI-native applications that rewrite enterprise workflows — with explicit interest in conversational AI, data-analytics agents, and agentic operations tooling.

**Background.** B.S. Management Science, UC San Diego; some graduate work in data science. Leo's operator credential is [Brewit](https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/brewit-data) (YC W23), which he co-founded with twin brothers Mark and Sam — a conversational data-analytics agent letting non-technical users query warehouses in natural language. He transitioned from founder to investor at Foundation, where he's now a Partner. Runs on an "I've been in your seat" pitch to founders.

**Relevance (quant lens).** Of the four, the most directly interesting. A VC partner whose explicit mandate is AI-native enterprise data tooling is a useful person to know for anyone building the read-side of a systematic research stack — natural-language-to-SQL, agent-driven ETL, research-automation tooling all sit in his strike zone. He's also an entry point to Foundation's broader fintech/data portfolio. Not a "call him about macro," but a "useful node in the AI-infra graph" kind of contact.
