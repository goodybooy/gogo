## 1. Statista — America's Top Incubators and Accelerators 2026

### What It Is
A joint ranking by **TIME** and **Statista** identifying the most effective startup incubators and accelerators in the United States. This is the inaugural edition of the list.

### Status
**Not yet published.** The final list will be released on time.com in **August 2026**. As of April 24, 2026, it is still in the survey/submission phase.

### Timeline
- Registration open: January 12 – May 1, 2026
- Alumni survey deadline: May 1, 2026
- Publication: August 2026

### Methodology
Three inputs determine inclusion:
- Self-reported program data (track record, structure, offerings)
- Alumni survey feedback from participants who went through programs between 2020–2025
- Input from experienced business-building professionals

### Eligibility Requirements
- Physically located in the United States
- Offers at least one incubation or acceleration program
- Has operated since at least 2022
- Can provide alumni access for evaluation surveys

### What Gets Published
Each ranked program's name, founding date, website, and location appear on time.com. Ranked organizations receive advance notification before public release. Using the official award logo requires a separate licensing fee (display on the list itself is free).

### Relevance
This list is positioned as a key resource for founders and investors to identify high-value early-stage programs — covering mentorship, business development, investor access, and infrastructure.

## 2. Salas O'Brien — Company Overview

### Basics
| | |
|---|---|
| **Founded** | 1975 |
| **HQ** | San Jose, California |
| **CEO** | Darin Anderson |
| **Founders** | Carl Salas, Roy Goodman, Dan O'Brien |
| **Type** | Private, majority employee-owned |
| **Employees** | 3,800+ (as of end-2024) |
| **ENR Ranking** | #39, Top 500 Design Firms (2024); #13 MEP Giants |

### What They Do
Salas O'Brien is a full-service engineering and technical services firm focused on the built environment. Core disciplines include:
- Mechanical, electrical, plumbing (MEP) engineering
- Fire protection
- Structural engineering
- Commissioning and energy auditing
- Telecom and technology design
- Clean energy / EPC services
- Construction management and facility planning

Key sectors: healthcare, education, government, data centers (including hyperscale AI), clean energy, and commercial.

### 2024 Performance Highlights
- **47% year-over-year net revenue increase**
- 8% organic growth
- 54% increase in post-bonus EBITDA
- $540M year-end backlog (12-month labor equivalent)
- Served 4,600 clients across 17,300 average annual projects
- $20B in annual construction value managed
- 91% team member retention rate
- 90% of employees are employee-owners
- Opened 16 new office locations; completed 7 mergers

### Blackstone Investment (January 2024)
Blackstone paid **$300 million** for a minority stake in Salas O'Brien. The company remains majority employee-owned. The investment is meant to fuel continued M&A-driven expansion in the AEC (architecture, engineering, construction) market.

### Recent Acquisitions (2024–2025)
- **BWK Engineering** (June 2025) — MEP, fire protection, and technology design
- **Lighting Design Alliance / LDA** (July 2025) — internationally recognized lighting design firm, Long Beach, CA
- **Carolina Engineering Solutions** (April 2025) — MEP design in Greenville, SC; strengthens education, healthcare, and government coverage
- 7 additional mergers completed in 2024 (names not all publicly disclosed)

### Employee Sentiment (Glassdoor — 161 reviews, ~3.9/5)
**Positives:**
- Strong employee-ownership culture; people feel invested in outcomes
- Good work-life balance for most roles
- Broad project variety — "never boring"
- Professional development programs (Impact Academy, licensing support)
- Competitive base pay for senior roles; wide salary range ($54K intern to $200K+ VP)

**Negatives / Concerns:**
- Management described as "disconnected" and more focused on acquisitions than day-to-day work
- Some reports of expectations for 12-hour days, 6 days/week with no overtime pay (framed as "learning opportunity")
- Compensation rating down ~6% over last 12 months
- Integration of acquisitions creates inconsistency across offices
- Bonuses don't always reflect performance

**Overall:** 68% of employees would recommend to a friend. Culture score 3.8/5, work-life balance 3.9/5, career opportunities 3.8/5, compensation 3.7/5.
