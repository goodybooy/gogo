Why it matters for your target seat: FX carry is the oldest and most-studied systematic macro factor, and understanding the DBV construction is the baseline from which every interesting variant is defined. A systematic macro desk will almost certainly ask you (a) how you'd build a carry basket, (b) why carry blew up in 2008 (DBV lost ~30% in a few weeks — the classic "carry unwind" where funding currencies like JPY and CHF ripped higher as risk-off forced de-leveraging), and (c) how you'd improve on naive DBV. The standard improvements are vol-targeting the basket, risk-parity across legs rather than equal notional, conditioning on a risk-off signal (VIX, FX vol, equity drawdown) to cut exposure before crashes, extending to EM carry with a separate sleeve, and generalizing beyond FX to the Koijen-Moskowitz-Pedersen-Vrugt "Carry" paper (2018), which defines carry across FX, rates, equities, commodities, and credit in one unified framework — that paper is the single best reference to read and is not in your folder.
So when the review says "EM carry baskets (DBV-style)" is missing, the gap is specifically: no dedicated material on (i) the mechanics of building a carry basket, (ii) the blowup risk and how to manage it, and (iii) extending it from G10 to EM (which is where your target role's "EM" focus actually lives).
